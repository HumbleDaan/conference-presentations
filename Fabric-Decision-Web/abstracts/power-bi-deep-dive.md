# The Fabric Decision Web: Making Implementation Choices That Match Your Organization

**Conference:** Power BI Deep Dive
**Session Length:** 3 hours
**Audience:** Advanced practitioners and implementation leaders

## Abstract

Most Fabric implementations force six critical decisions: capacity organization, workspace topology, data architecture, user enablement, COE structure, and governance. These decisions lack universal answers, and some lock you in while others evolve. Make the wrong choice early, and you'll spend months untangling consequences.

This deep dive navigates these interconnected decisions by exploring dependencies and what's hardest to change. With three hours, we'll work through real scenarios - source versus consumption architecture, how workspace topology affects governance, why some COE structures enable while others gatekeep.

Drawing on real examples, we'll examine common pitfalls: IT teams applying infrastructure mental models to SaaS governance, BI teams building source-driven architectures that prevent integration, organizations paralyzed by perfection, and how decisions interact to compound problems or create coherent systems.

You'll identify where your organization sits on the Business-IT spectrum, distinguish decisions needing planning (capacity topology, workspace boundaries) versus iteration (enablement, governance policies), and develop criteria fitting your reality - not idealized versions.

Bring your implementation challenges and questions.

## Target Audience

- Fabric capacity and tenant administrators planning or managing implementations
- Power BI platform owners transitioning to Fabric
- Center of Excellence leads designing enablement and governance strategies
- Analytics team leaders managing Fabric deployments
- Consultants advising on Fabric implementation approaches
- Anyone responsible for making (or living with) Fabric implementation decisions

## Key Takeaways

1. Comprehensive framework for navigating six critical Fabric implementation decisions and their interdependencies
2. Recognition patterns for common failure modes: infrastructure mental models applied to SaaS, source-driven architecture traps, paralysis-by-perfect-planning
3. Deep understanding of which decisions lock you in (capacity topology, workspace boundaries, data architecture) versus which can evolve (enablement, governance policies)
4. Practical criteria for choosing patterns based on your position on the Business-IT spectrum and organizational maturity
5. Decision-making strategies for real-world scenarios: brownfield migrations, hybrid IT/business control, evolving from starter patterns
6. Coherent implementation approaches across capacity, workspace, architecture, enablement, COE, and governance that actually fit together
