# Fabric Capacities: From Mechanics to Strategy

**Conference:** Internal (Microsoft FTE)
**Session Length:** TBD
**Audience:** Cloud Solution Architects, Data & AI specialists, technical sellers

## Abstract

We spend our days advising customers on Azure - PaaS services with familiar scaling models, per-use billing, and elastic compute. Then Fabric shows up with capacities, and the rules change. Bursting, smoothing windows, throttling instead of billing overages - it's a fundamentally different consumption model, and most of us aren't explaining it well because we don't fully understand it ourselves.

This session breaks down how Fabric capacities actually work - not the marketing version, but the mechanics. How consumption gets measured and smoothed. Why throttling happens when it does (and why customers think it's random). How the capacity model compares to what we're used to recommending in PaaS - and where that comparison breaks down. We'll walk through the Capacity Metrics App the way you'd need to in a customer conversation: diagnosing what's happening and knowing which levers to point them toward.

If you've ever been in a customer meeting where capacities came up and you quietly hoped someone else would answer - this is for you.

## Target Audience

- Cloud Solution Architects advising on Fabric or data platform strategy
- Data & AI specialists supporting Fabric engagements
- Technical sellers positioning Fabric alongside other Azure services
- Anyone who's been asked "why is my Fabric slow?" and didn't have a confident answer

## Key Takeaways

1. How the capacity consumption model works - bursting, smoothing, and throttling explained as mechanics, not metaphors
2. Where the Fabric model breaks from PaaS assumptions - and the misconceptions customers (and we) carry over
3. Reading the Capacity Metrics App with enough confidence to use it in a customer conversation
4. The levers that exist for optimization - and the ones customers think exist but don't
5. How to frame capacity guidance in customer-facing conversations without oversimplifying
