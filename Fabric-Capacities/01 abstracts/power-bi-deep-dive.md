# Fabric Capacities: From Mechanics to Strategy

**Conference:** Power BI Deep Dive
**Session Length:** 3 hours
**Audience:** Advanced Power BI practitioners and capacity administrators

## Abstract

Capacities underpin everything in Fabric, yet for many teams they remain a black box. You're getting throttled - but why? Migrating from Premium - will workloads behave the same? Overpaying - how would you even tell?

This deep dive gives you comprehensive understanding to master capacity management. We'll cover fundamentals - bursting, smoothing, workload charging - then build to advanced optimization. Using real Capacity Metrics App data, we'll walk through diagnosis patterns and what levers you can pull.

With three hours, we'll explore the different options at your disposal: ever thought about quotas and reservations? What's possible with surge protection and how doe sthe RTI implementation actually work? Let's find out together. 

Bring your questions and real-world scenarios.

## Target Audience

- Power BI and Fabric capacity administrators managing production environments
- Premium capacity administrators planning Fabric migrations
- Advanced practitioners wanting to understand platform behavior
- Consultants advising on capacity strategy
- Anyone who's stared at the Capacity Metrics App wondering "what does this actually mean?"

## Key Takeaways

1. Comprehensive understanding of capacity mechanics: bursting, smoothing windows, throttling behavior, and workload-specific consumption patterns
2. Mastery of the Capacity Metrics App: reading timelines, interpreting smoothing, diagnosing root causes of performance issues
3. Advanced optimization strategies: refresh scheduling, workload distribution, capacity topology decisions, and cost management
4. Premium to Fabric migration considerations: what changes, what stays the same, and how to plan the transition
5. Practical diagnosis patterns for common scenarios: mysterious throttling, unexpected costs, performance degradation
6. Understanding the levers you can pull: autoscale, capacity pausing, workload settings, and workspace assignment strategies
